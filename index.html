About

I am an undergraduate student in Mechanical Engineering and an undergraduate researcher at RVLab, Kyonggi University (advisor: Prof. Minseong Choi). I am fascinated by mobility — how machines perceive, decide, and move — and I like to understand these systems from the physical layer all the way up to the algorithm.

My interest began with mobility itself — the way physical systems move and interact with the world. This drew me toward vehicle hardware and embedded systems, where I built actuators, sensors, and MCU communication from the ground up. From there I moved into autonomous driving, designing steering and speed controllers, then expanded further into simulation and digital twins, running navigation and manipulation algorithms for humanoid and mobile robots in NVIDIA Isaac Sim. My current research moves into perception, studying ego-velocity estimation with 4D radar.

Across hardware, simulation, and sensing, I have come to see autonomy as one connected problem — from the actuator to the algorithm. For graduate study, I want to deepen this into rigorous research on perception and control for autonomous mobility.
